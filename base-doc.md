# A Curated Selection of Important Primary Sources in Computer Science & High Technology

This list is intended to track the historical development of the tech world as we know it now. Inclusion is not endorsement of the views presented.

Title | Author | Identifier | Publication date | Document format | Publisher/org | Topic 
-|-|-|-|-|-|-
[La Machine d’arithmétique. Lettre dédicatoire à Monseigneur le Chancelier, 1645](https://fr.wikisource.org/wiki/La_Machine_d%E2%80%99arithm%C3%A9tique) | by Blaise Pascal | | 1645 | Letter | | Pertains to the manufacture of the Pascaline, one of the first mechanical calculators of the modern age.
[Note G, or Diagram for the computation by the Engine of the Numbers of Bernoulli, from Sketch of The Analytical Engine Invented by Charles Babbage](https://www.gutenberg.org/files/75107/75107-h/75107-h.htm) | by Luigi Federico Menabrea, translated by Ada Lovelace | | 1843 | Essay | | The first published algorithm written for a computer.
[As We May Think](https://web.mit.edu/STS.035/www/PDFs/think.pdf) |  by Vannevar Bush | | July 1945 | Article | The Atlantic Monthly | Introduces Memex, an early concept for a document-linking system similar to Hypertext.
[Computing Machinery and Intelligence]() | by Alan Turing | | 1950 | Paper | Mind: A Quarterly Review of Philosophy | Introduces the Turing Test.
[Dartmouth Summer Research Project on Artificial Intelligence](https://raysolomonoff.com/dartmouth/boxa/dart564props.pdf) | by J. McCarthy, M.L. Minsky, N. Rochester, C.E. Shannon | | 1956 | Grant proposal | Dartmouth | Coined the term, "artificial intelligence".
[Recursive Functions of Symbolic Expressions and Their Computation by Machine, Part I](http://jmc.stanford.edu/articles/recursive/recursive.pdf) | by John McCarthy | | 1958 | Paper | MIT | The first paper on LISP, the second-oldest high-level programming language still in common use.
[Prof. Nelson Talk Analyzes P.R.I.D.E.](https://web.archive.org/web/20130510074431/http://faculty.vassar.edu/mijoyce/MiscNews_Feb65.html) | by Lauren Wedeles (reporting on statements from Theodor Nelson) | | February 1965 | Article | Vassar College Miscellany News | First use in print of "Hypertext".
[Flow Diagrams, Turing Machines And Languages With Only Two Formation Rules](https://www.cs.unibo.it/~martini/PP/bohm-jac.pdf) | by Corrado Bohm and Giuseppe Jacopini | | May 1966 | Paper | | Introduces the structured programming theorem, which lead to software development favoring loops and subroutines over GO TO jump statements.
[A Case against the GO TO Statement, or Go-to statement considered harmful](https://www.cs.utexas.edu/~EWD/transcriptions/EWD02xx/EWD215.html) | by Edsger Dijkstra | | 1968 | Letter | Technological University | Influential criticism of unstructured programming.
[The Mother of All Demos](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=UhpTiWyVa6k&list=PLCGFadV4FqU3flMPLg36d8RFQW65bWsnP&t=12s) | by Doug Engelbart & Team | | 1968 | Video | | The introduction of the keyboard, monitor, and mouse.
[A Research Center for Augmenting Human Intellect](https://dougengelbart.org/content/view/140/) | by Douglas C. Engelbart and William K. English | | Dec 9 1968 | Paper | Published in AFIPS Conference Proceedings of the 1968 Fall Joint Computer Conference, San Francisco, CA, December 9, 1968, Vol. 33, pp. 395-410 | Accompanying paper for "Mother of All Demos".
[A Relational Model of Data for Large Shared Data Banks](https://dl.acm.org/doi/10.1145/362384.362685) | by Edgar Codd | | 1970 | Paper | Communications of the ACM, Volume 13, Issue 6 | Introduces relational databases and the basis for SQL.
[The Elements of Programming Style](https://archive.org/details/elementsofprogra00kernrich) | by Brian W. Kernighan and P.J. Plauger | | 1974 | Book | | Early book on general programming best practices.
AIWORD.RF[UP,DOC], aka [The Jargon File](https://jargon-file.org/archive/), The Hacker's Dictionary, or The New Hacker's Dictionary | by numerous contributers | | 1975 to 2003 | Digital text file | |Influential work on computer programmer humor and culture. We include the original file, plus the text of the books, *The Hacker's Dictionary* and *The New Hacker's Dictionary*.
[The Mythical Man-Month: Essays on Software Engineering]() | by Fred Brooks | ISBN 	9780201006506 | 1975 | Book | | Influential book on project management of software teams; coined "10x engineer".
[Hypertext: an Educational Experiment in English and Computer Science at Brown University](https://archive.org/details/AndyVanDamHypertextFilm) | by Andries van Dam | | 1976 | Video | | Documentary on the first hypertext community of writers.
[Aspen Interactive Movie Map](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Hf6LkqgXPMU) | by team lead by Andrew B. Lippman | | 1981 (first operational(?) 1979) | Video | MIT | An interactive video project mapping all of the streets of Aspen, Colorado, complete with 3D models, zoom, and metadata about individual buildings; similar to Google Maps.
[Mindstorms](https://www.media.mit.edu/publications/mindstorms/) | by Seymour A. Papert | ISBN 9781541675124 | 1980 | Book | | Influential book on computers in education, inspiring numerous projects including the Lego Mindstorms playsets.
[IEN 137: ON HOLY WARS AND A PLEA FOR PEACE](https://www.rfc-editor.org/ien/ien137.txt) | by Danny Cohen | | 1980 | Digital text file | | First use in print of "Endianness" for byte order.
[The Transaction Concept: Virtues and Limitations](https://jimgray.azurewebsites.net/papers/theTransactionConcept.pdf?from=https://research.microsoft.com/~gray/papers/theTransactionConcept.pdf&type=path) | by Jim Gray | | 1981 | Paper | Tandem TR 81.3 | Describes the concept of transactions (used for data management) and introduces ACID (Atomic, Consistent, Isolated, Durable) as defining traits for good transaction design.
[Epigrams on Programming](https://iiif.library.cmu.edu/file/Simon_box00075_fld05959_bdl0003_doc0002/Simon_box00075_fld05959_bdl0003_doc0002.pdf) aka "Perlisisms" | by Alan J. Perlis | | 1982 | Article |  SIGPLAN at Yale | Widely quoted aphorisms; first print usage of "Turing tarpit".
[Principles of transaction-oriented database recovery](https://dl.acm.org/doi/pdf/10.1145/289.291) | by Andreas Reuter and Theo Härder | | 1983 | Paper | ACM Computing Surveys | Introduces the ACID acronym.
[The Design of Everyday Things]() | by Donald Norman | | 1988 | Book | | Classic work on user-centered design.
[Information Management: A Proposal](https://www.w3.org/History/1989/proposal.html) | by Tim Berners-Lee | | 1989, 1990 | Digital text file | CERN |  Proposal for a global network of documents connected via hypertext -- a worldwide web, if you will.
[afternoon, a story](https://www.eastgate.com/catalog/Afternoon.html) | by Michael Joyce | | 1990 | Digital text file | | The first hypertext novel.
[Archie](https://archie.serialport.org/) | | | 1990 | Server-side software | | The first Internet search engine, designed for finding files uploaded to FTP archives.
[Design Patterns: Elements of Reusable Object-Oriented Software]() | by Erich Gamma, Richard Helm, Ralph Johnson, John Vlissides | ISBN 0201485370 | 1994 | Book | | Classic work on object-oriented programming design.
[Artificial Intelligence: A Modern Approach](https://people.engr.tamu.edu/guni/csce625/slides/AI.pdf) | by Stuart J. Russel & Peter Norvig | | 1995 | Book | The standard textbook on artificial intelligence for the past 30 years.
[The Cathedral and the Bazaar: Musings on Linux and Open Source by an Accidental Revolutionary]() | by Eric S. Raymond | | 1998, 1999 | Book (originally digital text file) | | Influential work on software engineering methods and open source; inspired the Mozilla project.
Don't Make Think aka [Don't Make Me Think, Revisited](https://archive.org/details/dontmakemethinkr0000krug) | by Steve Krug | | 2000, 2014 | Book | | Classic book on UX design principles for web and (later) mobile apps.
[Architectural Styles and the Design of Network-based Software Architectures](https://ics.uci.edu/~fielding/pubs/dissertation/top.htm) | by Roy Thomas Fielding |  | 2000 | Paper | University of California, Irvine | First print usage of "REST" and "HATEOAS"; describes basis for REST APIs.
